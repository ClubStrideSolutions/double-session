Intro to Public Health & YPAR 25 mins

  • Foundational public health concepts
  • Why community needs assessments matter
  • Key principles of YPAR
+

Key Terms

Public Health: The science of protecting and improving community health.

Community Needs Assessment: Identifying local issues to guide solutions.

Youth Participatory Action Research (YPAR): Engaging youth as co-researchers.

Discussion Points

  • How can public health data shape community programs or policies?
  • Why is a needs assessment critical before launching a project?
  • How does YPAR differ from traditional research approaches?

Activity

Analyze a brief case study showing how youth researchers used public health data to address a school-based health issue. Discuss possible parallels in your local context.

Reflection Questions

  • What public health issues resonate most with you?
  • How do you see youth involvement affecting real change in these issues?

Quantitative vs. Qualitative Methods 15 mins

  • When to use each approach
  • Strengths & limitations
  • Potential AI integrations
+

Key Terms

Quantitative Research: Collecting numerical data for statistical analysis.

Qualitative Research: Exploring themes and experiences through interviews or focus groups.

AI Integration: Tools for data analysis or question generation based on large datasets.

Discussion Points

  • Which local problems might be better understood with quantitative data?
  • What role can qualitative stories or interviews play in shaping policy?
  • How might AI streamline collecting or interpreting this data?

Activity

Work with a partner to brainstorm 2–3 hypothetical research questions: at least one fits a quantitative approach (e.g., surveys) and one fits a qualitative approach (e.g., interviews).

Reflection Questions

  • Which method feels most accessible to you right now?
  • How could mixing methods provide a fuller picture of a community issue?

Team-Building: “What are the needs in our community?” 20 mins

  • Brainstorming local issues
  • Digital whiteboarding session
  • Identify potential research topics
+

Key Terms

Brainstorming: Generating ideas freely without immediate judgment.

Collaborative Mapping: Visualizing community issues as a team.

Digital Whiteboard: Online tools for real-time, shared note-taking or sketching.

Discussion Points

  • Why is group input valuable for identifying community issues?
  • How might digital tools enhance or hinder this process?
  • What themes keep surfacing across different groups?

Activity

In small groups, create virtual “sticky notes” listing local challenges you’re aware of. Group similar notes to discover overarching themes. Discuss how AI might help gather more data on those themes.

Reflection Questions

  • Which community needs did you find surprising?
  • Did discussing with others change your perspective on local issues?

Pre-Training Knowledge Check 20 mins

  • Short quiz on research basics
  • Assess familiarity with AI tools
  • Identify areas for growth
+

Key Terms

Knowledge Check: A preliminary assessment of your current skill set.

Baseline Understanding: Your starting point before formal training.

Self-Assessment: Reflecting on your knowledge gaps or strengths.

Discussion Points

  • How do knowledge checks help shape future learning activities?
  • Which topics are you most/least confident in?
  • How can we use AI to address specific knowledge gaps?

Activity

Complete a 10-question survey (digital or paper) covering basic concepts of public health, YPAR, and AI usage. Discuss any confusing questions with a peer or mentor afterward.

Reflection Questions

  • Which quiz areas showed the greatest need for more training?
  • How might you use AI or additional resources to improve your understanding?
